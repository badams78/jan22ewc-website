Description

This subcluster focuses on the H5N1 avian influenza outbreak and its escalating public health implications, tracking viral transmission across species barriers from birds to livestock and humans. Articles predominantly cite CDC data, WHO reports, and epidemiological studies, documenting infection rates in dairy herds, human cases, and emerging viral variants like D1.1. Coverage emphasizes surveillance data, serologic surveys, and genomic sequencing results. Unlike sibling subclusters examining energy infrastructure and scientific breakthroughs, this group addresses immediate biosecurity threats and disease containment challenges, representing a critical intersection of agricultural economics and pandemic preparedness within broader infrastructure resilience discussions.
